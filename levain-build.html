Levain Build Calculator

Work backwards from when you want to mix your dough: find out when to build your levain, at what ratio, and how much of each ingredient to use.

What a levain build ratio means

A levain is an offshoot of your starter, built specifically for one bake. The ratio describes how much mature starter seeds the build relative to fresh flour and water: a 1:5:5 build uses 1 part starter to 5 parts each of flour and water. Higher ratios (more fresh food per unit of starter) take longer to peak, because the small starter population has more territory to colonize — which is exactly why they're useful for overnight schedules.

Timing and temperature

Temperature moves levain timing the same way it moves dough fermentation — roughly twice as fast for every 15-17°F warmer. This calculator adjusts the expected peak window for your room temperature. A levain is ready when it has domed and roughly doubled or tripled, is full of bubbles, and smells pleasantly tangy; if it has collapsed and smells sharply acidic, it's past peak — still usable, but your dough may ferment faster and taste more sour.

Once your levain is mixed into the dough, plan the rest of your schedule with the fermentation calculator, or set up an overnight schedule with the cold retard calculator.
