How this calculator works

Fermentation speed in sourdough is driven mainly by temperature, since wild yeast and bacteria activity roughly doubles for every 15-17°F increase. This calculator factors in both your dough's starting temperature and the room it will sit in, since dough gradually drifts toward room temperature over the course of a long ferment, especially in a thin bowl or a drafty kitchen. Starter percentage shifts timing because more starter means more active microorganisms working from the start, and hydration has a smaller effect: wetter doughs ferment slightly faster because enzymes and microbes move more freely through more water.

This tool gives an estimate, not a guarantee. The right stopping point for bulk fermentation is really about dough behavior: look for it to have grown by 30-75% in volume, feel airy and jiggly, and show some bubbles on the surface, rather than relying on the clock alone.

Planning an overnight bake?

Tips for consistent results

Use the same flour and starter routine each time you bake, since strain and flour type affect speed too. If your kitchen temperature swings a lot, consider proofing in the oven with the light on, or in a turned-off microwave, to get a more stable environment.
